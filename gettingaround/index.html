Getting Around


General info

In Tallinn everything is easy to reach by public transport (buses, trolleys, trams), taxi or ridesharing apps. A tram (line no. 4) is also a good option to get from the airport to the city centre.

The public transport network operates broadly from 6:00 to 23:00 daily (some lines until 24:00, see here). You do not have to show anything to the driver upon entry to the vehicle, but you should validate your pre-purchased ticket at an orange validator, located near the entrance. It is not necessary to re-validate your ticket when exiting. Due to the coronavirus, it is currently not possible to buy a ticket from the driver. We therefore recommend you buy QR-tickets on the internet, or single tickets at the ticket machine located in the Tallinn airport tram waiting hall and at R-Kiosks all over the city (1.50€). Alternatively, you can buy a Smartcard (Ühiskaart, 2.00€) when you arrive at the airport. You can then top the card up at any point and it can be used for up to six passengers. You can buy the card from the R-Kiosk located in the open area of the airport. You can find all the relevant information on public transport here and here.

An easy and a relatively inexpensive way to get around is to use a ridesharing or a taxi-ordering app, such as Bolt or Uber. Both use professional and/or verified private taxi drivers. You will have to install a relevant app on your mobile phone and the rest is easy. You simply enter your starting location and the destination, and the app displays to you all the available cars as well as their rates. Regular taxis can be found in front of the airport, larger hotels, etc., and can also be ordered by phone (e.g. here). Taxi rates in Tallinn are not uniform and can vary quite widely so make sure to familiarise yourself with the pricelist on the car window before getting in. The cost typically consists of a fixed starting fare and a kilometre rate.

For shorter distances renting a scooter might also be a good option, see e.g. here.

Getting to the festivity

BOTIK baar is located at Marati 5 in Kopli in the Northeast of Tallinn (see map). The place is only 200 meters from tram stop “Marati” on the 1 and 2 lines. There is a tram going there every 8-9 minutes on a Saturday. The last tram back to the city centre departs at around midnight but Bolt and Uber (see above) offer fairly cheap and comfortable alternatives both to and from the venue.
